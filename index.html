about me

I am currently in my 2nd year, heading into my 3rd year next winter (due to credits from Cégep). I'm looking for COOP opportunities this next summer in the webdev and robotics/IoT industries.

As for now, I am VP Social & Communications at uOttawa SESA (Software Engineering Student Association). I've been part of this organization from my first year, during which I worked mainly on translating communications from English to French and communicating events organized by the organization to our fellow engineering students.

Outside of school, I like to read about history, political sciences and technology, whether it is in physical books, newspapers, or HackerNews and Medium. I follow online courses on platforms like edX and Codecademy in order to learn new technologies and programming languages, or engineering/science subjects that interest me - for example, I'll soon achieve an astronomy course on edX -.

Also, I like to play hockey - I'm a defenseman -, watch soccer (German fan here) and to play the drums. My favourite bands would be Rush (you know... Tom Sawyer? Or if you dare, 2112... yeah, it's 20mins long, but you gotta do what you gotta do!), Nirvana (and pretty much all other grunge/alt rock of the 90s) and Jean Leloup (yay, good stuff from Québec).

education & skills

  • University of Ottawa (2014-2018) - GPA: 9.4

    Bachelor of Applied Science (B.A.Sc.) Software Engineering

    So far, I've studied the basis of programming, engineering and digital systems. This year will be more thorough, as I'll dig in software engineering practices, advanced concepts of C++, databases and computer architecture.

    If all goes according to plan, I'll be part of the Undergraduate Research Opportunity Program (UROP) this year, in which I'll be supervised by Dr. Pierre Payeur. My task will be to work with a robotic hand (BarrettHand) in a context of manipulation of deformable materials; I'll be working on the software, and on the collection of essential data for a fellow Ph.D. student who will need it for his thesis. More details to come, later in the school year.
